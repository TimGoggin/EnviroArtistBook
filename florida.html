Half of the Original Everglades Have Been Destroyed

Many Florida politicians have ran campaigns on destroying the Everglades to make way for space that is easier to farm and build on. This has resulted in over half of the original Everglades being destroyed for agricultural purposes. This is an example of habitat destruction.

What Remains Has Been Polluted

Agricultural and industry runoffs have polluted the waters of the Everglades to dangerous levels. This is an example of habitat degradation.

Extinction

The Everglades is home to 350 species, over 10% of which are endangered or critically endangered. This includes the Florida Panther, which has been driven to near extinction.
